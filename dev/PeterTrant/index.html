Biography

Peter Trant studied Drama in Dublin and London in the 1970's.

A member of The Drumlin Players, roles performed include: Michael in Dancing at Lughnasa; Eddie in A View from the Bridge; Roy in Lone Star; and Senator Doogan in Philadelphia Here I Come. For the Garage Theatre he appeared as Juror No. 8 in Twelve Angry Men.

In 2007 The Revenant, a one-man play written by Patrick McCabe and directed by Joe O'Byrne was performed by Peter in The Druid Theatre as part of The Galway Arts Festival. It was a huge success. The play was also performed in 2010 at the The Edinburgh Festival.

Peter regularly tours with Patrick McCabe's Radio Butty, where he plays a variety of characters including the deluded Larry Bunyan - insurance agent and would-be hitman from Barntrosna - and the inscrutable H. McGeough from Mullingar, Bruce Lee's best mate. He has also devised and performed with actress Krista Rushing-Hamilton a piece entitled 'And Your Eyes…A Crush of Diamonds' dealing with the lives and work of Ted Hughes and Sylvia Plath. 'Larry Bunyan's' last appearance was at The Flatlake Festival in County Monaghan in June 2011.
